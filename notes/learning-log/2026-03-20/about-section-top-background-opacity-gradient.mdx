## Context

You wanted the About section background to read with **reduced opacity at the top** so the scroll handoff from terrain feels softer and more layered.

## Goal

Replace a flat `#0a0a0f` fill with a vertical gradient: semi-transparent at the top, solid brand dark at the bottom.

## Files Changed

- `components/about-section.tsx`

## Implementation Notes (key logic explained)

- Removed `bg-[#0a0a0f]` from the `<section>`.
- Applied `style.background` with `linear-gradient(to bottom, …)`:
  - **Top:** `rgba(10, 10, 15, 0.45)` (~45% opacity) so content behind (e.g. sticky terrain) can show through.
  - **Mid:** ramps through `0.82` opacity then reaches full `#0a0a0f` by ~38% of section height.
  - **Rest:** solid `#0a0a0f` for readable body copy.

## Key Decisions (and alternatives considered)

- **Decision:** gradient on the section itself (under dot grid + optional terrain blend).
- **Alternative:** extra absolutely positioned overlay — rejected to avoid duplicating background logic.

## Assumptions

- `rgba(10, 10, 15, …)` matches your existing `#0a0a0f` hue.

## Failure Modes

- If used on a light parent (no terrain), the top may look washed — only mount with dark stack or tune stops.

## How to Test (commands + steps)

1. `npm run dev` → `/terrain`, scroll into About — top should feel more “airy” and blended.

## Next Steps

- Tweak `0.45` / `0.82` / `38%` stops if you want stronger or subtler transparency.
